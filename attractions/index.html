Explore Yogyakarta

Discover the rich cultural heritage, natural beauty, and unique experiences in and around Yogyakarta.

Cultural Heritage

Yogyakarta is the cultural heart of Java, with a rich history reflected in its ancient temples, royal palaces, and traditional arts.

Borobudur Temple

40 km northwest of Yogyakarta

The world's largest Buddhist temple, dating from the 9th century. This UNESCO World Heritage site features stunning reliefs and Buddha statues arranged around a central stupa.

Best time to visit: Early morning for sunrise

Prambanan Temple

17 km northeast of Yogyakarta

A magnificent Hindu temple complex built in the 9th century. This UNESCO World Heritage site features tall, pointed structures dedicated to the Trimurti: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva.

Best time to visit: Late afternoon or for the Ramayana Ballet performances

Keraton (Sultan's Palace)

Yogyakarta City Center

The palace of the Yogyakarta Sultanate, a model of traditional Javanese architecture. Visitors can explore the grounds, see the sultan's artifacts, and sometimes witness traditional performances.

Best time to visit: Mornings, when cultural performances are often held

Natural Beauty

From volcanic landscapes to pristine beaches, Yogyakarta offers diverse natural attractions for outdoor enthusiasts.

Mount Merapi

28 km north of Yogyakarta

One of Indonesia's most active volcanoes, offering hiking trails, jeep tours, and stunning views. The Merapi Museum provides insight into past eruptions and local geology.

Best time to visit: Early morning for clear views

Parangtritis Beach

27 km south of Yogyakarta

A famous black sand beach along the southern coast of Java. Visitors can ride horses along the shore, take dune buggy rides, or watch the sunset over the Indian Ocean.

Best time to visit: Late afternoon for the sunset

Pindul Cave

Gunungkidul, 40 km east of Yogyakarta

Experience cave tubing through an underground river, guided by expert locals. The cave features impressive stalactite and stalagmite formations illuminated by natural light.

Best time to visit: Weekdays to avoid crowds

Arts & Crafts

Yogyakarta is famous for its vibrant arts scene, traditional crafts, and cultural performances.

Batik Workshops

Various locations in Yogyakarta

Learn the traditional art of batik-making, where intricate patterns are created using wax and dyes. Many workshops offer hands-on experiences where you can create your own batik piece.

Recommendation: Book a workshop at least a day in advance

Silver Crafts in Kotagede

Kotagede, southeastern Yogyakarta

Visit the historic neighborhood of Kotagede, known for its skilled silversmiths. Watch artisans create intricate jewelry and decorative items, and purchase unique souvenirs directly from local workshops.

Recommendation: Take a guided walking tour to learn about the area's history

Wayang Puppet Shows

Various venues in Yogyakarta

Experience the traditional Indonesian puppet theater, where shadow puppets perform epic stories accompanied by gamelan music. These performances showcase ancient tales from Hindu epics and Javanese mythology.

Recommendation: Check local event listings for performance schedules

Culinary Experiences

Yogyakarta offers a diverse culinary scene, from traditional Javanese dishes to modern fusion cuisine.

Malioboro Street Food

Malioboro Street, Yogyakarta City Center

Explore the bustling food stalls along Yogyakarta's famous shopping street. Try local specialties like gudeg (young jackfruit stew), bakpia (sweet filled pastries), and various satay dishes.

Best time to visit: Evening when the street food scene comes alive

Traditional Javanese Restaurants

Various locations in Yogyakarta

Enjoy authentic Javanese cuisine in traditional settings. Many restaurants serve meals on banana leaves or in clay pots, offering dishes like nasi gudeg, soto ayam (chicken soup), and sambal-based specialties.

Must try: Gudeg Yogya and Ayam Goreng Kalasan

Cooking Classes

Various locations in Yogyakarta

Learn to prepare authentic Javanese dishes in hands-on cooking classes. Many include market tours to select fresh ingredients, followed by preparation and a communal meal of the dishes you've created.

Recommendation: Book a small group class for more personalized instruction
